Research Experience

WorkflowLLM: Enhancing Workflow Orchestration Capability of Large Language Models

view publication

This 2024 project aims to build a database benchmark based on the huge workflow files in Apple's shortcuts software to facilitate the training of LLM construction workflows. I contributed to establishing a robust database by systematically collecting and organizing extensive workflow files from Apple’s Shortcuts software. My primary responsibilities included the statistical decoding of these source files and assisting in the data generation process, which supported the training of large language model construction workflows. This experience deepened my skills in data management and quantitative analysis, directly supporting advancements in LLM research.

A Comprehensive Research about Multi-Robot Control Models

view publication

This project in 2023 aims to use machine learning as the general direction. my team and I independently selected and investigated multi-robot control models. As the team leader, I orchestrated the research process by conducting comparative analyses of various control methodologies and authoring detailed technical documentation. Additionally, I coordinated team assignments and monitored progress to ensure timely completion of our objectives. This leadership role not only advanced my understanding of multi-robot systems but also refined my project management and collaborative skills.

A performance study of algorithms and frameworks for federated learning

view publication

During this project, I engaged in an intensive one-week offline research training on IoT technology, followed by independent research culminating in academic paper writing. This project significantly broadened my understanding of distributed machine learning techniques and their applications within IoT systems, while also enhancing my research and analytical capabilities.
